Tacos Gobernador

    Home

Description

These tacos originated in Mazatlan, Sinaloa, Mexico. It is said that the governor of Sinaloa loved shrimp and quesadillas so the chef of a well-known restaurant created these, a cross between a shrimp taco and a quesadilla. And so these were named Tacos Gobernador ('Governor Tacos')

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons salted butter
  • ⅓ cup diced onion
  • 1 serrano pepper, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 pound raw shrimp - peeled, deveined, and cut into small pieces
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • 4 teaspoons bacon drippings, divided
  • 8 (8inch) corn tortillas
  • 1 ½ cups shredded Oaxaca cheese

Steps

  1. Melt butter in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add onions and serrano and cook until onions are soft and translucent, 4 to 5 minutes.
  2. Add garlic and cook until fragran, about 1 minute.
  3. Season with salt and pepper. Add tomatoes to the skillet and cook until they have softened, about 3 minutes.
  4. Add shrimp and lime juice. Cook until shrimp turns pink, 2 to 3 minutes.
  5. Season with more salt and pepper.
  6. Remove from heat.
  7. Divide shrim mixture into 8 equal portions. Set aside.
  8. Melt 1/2 teaspoon bacon drippings in a cast iron skillet over medium-low heat.
  9. Place 1 tortilla on a bacon drippings.
  10. Add 1 1/2 tablespoons cheese and 1 portion shrimp mixture to one side of the tortilla.
  11. Fold over and cook until tortilla is semi-crisp and cheese has started to melt, 3 to 4 minutes.
  12. Flip taco onto other side and cook to desired crispiness, 2 to 3 minutes more.
  13. Repeat with remaining tortillas.
